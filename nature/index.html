Nature

Cedars Park is biodiverse with a strong mix of grassland, wildflower meadows and high-rise woodland that contributes to a very good environment for most native species that reside here. Broxbourne Borough Council considers the wildlife of the park in the annual Management and Maintenance Plan.

Birds

Many different bird species and varieties have been observed within Cedars Park. These include the native British species that are typically found in parks, but we are also aware of several exotic types, including Indian and Australian parakeets, that reside in our park.

Trees

Cedars Park contains a mature and diverse collection of trees from around the world. Between 2019 and 2021, volunteers from the Friends of Cedars Park identified and numbered all of the main trees in the park, allowing people to easily see the species of each tree and effectively creating a formal arboretum with a tree listing.

Tree identification

CPRE Hertfordshire said: 'The tree project is a valuable asset helping visitors to discover the trees in the park. Their species lists and measurements . . . have contributed to our national knowledge of trees in cultivation'.

To see the species of all trees in the arboretum, refer to the Cedars Park Arboretum Tree Listing.

Note: This Tree Listing only covers trees within the designated Arboretum Zones, which cover the majority of the park. If you would like to identify a tree outside of this area, such as in heavy woodland or the Wildflower Meadows, we have produced a basic Tree Identification Guide.

As part of the tree project, we have also collated some Tree Fun Facts to complement this information.
