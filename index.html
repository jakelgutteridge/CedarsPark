Welcome to Cedars Park

Cedars Park is a public park covering 19 acres in Cheshunt, Hertfordshire. It is the former site of Theobalds Palace, one of the most renowned English palaces of the Stuart period and King James I's favourite residence, where he spent the majority of his later life. The palace was demolished in the 17th century and many elements (Grade II listed) are still standing.

Today, Cedars Park is a very popular and attractive green space for the people of Cheshunt and the wider public who visit. You can get an impression of the park and its features from our photo gallery.

Friends of Cedars Park

The Friends of Cedars Park (FOCP) are a voluntary group established in 2005 to help maintain and improve the park. See the Friends page for more information.

This website has been created by the Friends Group to provide useful information to the public about the park and document the important history of Theobalds Palace. This is where we will publish information from projects that we carry out. For updates on events and activities, please visit us on Facebook.
