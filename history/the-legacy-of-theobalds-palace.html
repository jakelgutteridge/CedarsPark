The Legacy of Theobalds Palace

The significance of Theobalds Palace (now Cedars Park) is reflected in the surrounding area today. Here are some local links:

A remaining wall from Theobalds Palace
  • Theobalds Lane – Site of the main buildings.
  • Theobalds Brook – A minor tributary of the River Lea passes through the former palace grounds.
  • Cheshunt South & Theobalds – There is an entire area of Cheshunt known as 'Theobalds', showing the prominence of the palace.
  • Hedworth Avenue and Lambton Avenue – Named after Admiral of the Fleet Sir Hedworth Meux (n√© Lambton), who lived at Theobalds Park and donated Cedars Park to the people of Cheshunt in 1919.
  • Winston Churchill Way – Located immediately south of Cedars Park, this section of dual carriageway is named after the former Prime Minister. Lady Meux is known to have entertained him in the upper chamber of Temple Bar, when it was located at Theobalds Park.
  • King and Tinker – The early 17th century ballad of The King and the Tinker describes King James I becoming separated from his courtiers while out on a hunt at Theobalds and taking refuge in an alehouse, where he enjoyed a drink with a tinker. The tinker only realised who his drinking partner was when the courtiers caught up with the King. This meeting took place at the pub on Whitewebbs Lane, Enfield — now called The King and Tinker — which stood on the extensive deer hunting grounds of Theobalds Palace.
