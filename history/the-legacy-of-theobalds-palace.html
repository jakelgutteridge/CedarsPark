The Legacy of Theobalds Palace

The significance of Theobalds Palace (now Cedars Park) is reflected in the surrounding area today. Here are some local links:

A remaining wall from Theobalds Palace
  • Theobalds Lane – Site of the main buildings.
  • Theobalds Brook – A minor tributary of the River Lea passes through the former palace grounds.
  • Hedworth Avenue and Lambton Avenue – Named after Admiral of the Fleet Sir Hedworth Meux (n√© Lambton), who lived at Theobalds Park and donated Cedars Park to the people of Cheshunt in 1919.
  • Winston Churchill Way – Located immediately south of Cedars Park, this section of dual carriageway is named after the late Prime Minister. Lady Meux is known to have entertained him in the upper chamber of Temple Bar, when it was located at Theobalds Park.
  • The King and Tinker – Located on Whitewebbs Lane in Enfield, this historic pub dates back to the 16th century and is located on King James I's original hunting grounds. Their business card tells the story of the Ballad of the King and the Tinker:

    Whilst hunting in the area and separated from his courtiers the king takes refuge in an alehouse where he enjoys a drink with a tinker. The tinker has no idea who he is drinking with until the courtiers catch up with the king and the tinker realises who his drinking companion really is, King James the 1st.

    It is said that the King knighted the tinker and that this is the pub where their meeting took place.
